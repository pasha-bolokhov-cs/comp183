Instructions

Lab 1

For demonstration before the end of your labs session in week 3

20 Marks

Topics

  • CHASM
  • the CRAPS Debugger

Purpose

  • To become familiar with the CRAPS debugging system and basic concepts of assembly language. 

Assignment

Good morning (or afternoon) Ladies and Gentlemen.

Attached to this Dropbox item you will find a source program, lab1.csm which you are to use in this lab. Note that this is the only place where you will be able to download this file.  Because in this lab we will be working on Deepblue, you will be responsible for copying it to an appropriate location (using WinSCP or the like).

This program has several deliberate mistakes; there is at least one fatal typo (not a tough one to find) and a few logic errors.

Your assignment (should you choose to accept it or not) is firstly to get the program to assemble with no errors and no warnings, then to identify and fix the problems.

FYI, the correct output of the program is:

	Hello there!
	Bye now!

You will need to confirm that the arithmetic instructions and the loads and stores worked correctly by using display commands to check the registers and the contents of memory.

You must be able to answer the following questions (or show me how to find out) when you demo the lab:

  • What are the addresses of W1, W2 and W3?
  • What are the (hexadecimal) contents of W1, W2 and W3 after the program has run?
  • Which flags are on/off immediately after the add instruction?

Resources

A general introduction to using unix (and hence linux) can be found here; this is somewhat out of date (speak to its author) and contains far more material than you need to complete the COMP183 labs.

Information on the CRAB (CRAPS BIOS) will be found in the CRAB Reference. 

Hand In

Nothing! -ish...  As in COMP 176, your COMP183 assignments will be marked by your demonstrating them to me during your lab session. This not only means showing me that the program works, but being able to explain the program (or parts of it) when I ask.

Here is what you need to do in your demo:

  1. Run your program and show that its output is correct (e.g. craps lab1)
  2. Load your program into the debugger and then:
  3. Set a break point immediately after the add instructions
  4. Run the program up to the breakpoint
  5. Display the flags and the registers
  6. Let the program run to completion then display the contents of W1, W2 and W3 in hexadecimal and signed decimal.

You will need to submit you source code after having demonstrated it via this Dropbox.  You will loose marks if:

  1. Your source code is NOT submitted by the close of the lab availability window.
  2. You have not included your name, student ("C...") number, the date and time you demonstrated your lab, and at least one line containing the unique identifier I will give you when you have completed your demonstration.

Note that although this lab is to be demonstrated during week 3, there will be another lab assignment published before then. This is not a two-week lab.
